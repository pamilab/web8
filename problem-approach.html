Problem Context & Methodology

Bridging the gap between high-cost commercial radar and accessible research tools.

The Problem We Address

Subsurface inspection is critical in civil engineering, disaster response, and underground mapping. However, existing GPR systems are often expensive, bulky, and difficult to customize.

  • ✔ Affordable for institutions and researchers
  • ✔ Simple to deploy and operate
  • ✔ Reliable in controlled environments

How the System Works

1. Transmission

A frequency-swept FMCW chirp is transmitted toward the ground.

2. Reflection

Signals reflect and scatter from subsurface discontinuities.

3. Mixing

The received signal is mixed with the transmitted chirp to produce a beat frequency.

4. Digitization

Beat signals are digitized using an SDR platform.

5. STFT Processing

STFT transforms time-domain signals into depth-resolved frequency components.

6. Visualization

Data is visualized as radargrams showing subsurface structure.
